# Research Route

Use this mode when the learner wants to prepare a research path, reproduce ideas, identify open problems, or migrate paper understanding into a system.

## Required Separation

Keep four lanes separate:

- Understand: concepts, papers, methods, evidence.
- Reproduce: experiments, datasets, code, metrics, expected outputs.
- Question: unknowns, contradictions, open problems, missing evidence.
- Transfer: what can or cannot apply to the learner's system.

## Outputs

- Reading path ordered by prerequisite structure, not by popularity.
- Concept graph with dependencies.
- Evidence/source matrix for strong claims.
- Open-problem list with uncertainty labels.
- Migration plan only after the evidence chain is understood.

## Research Hygiene

- Prefer primary sources and official repositories.
- Mark survey/blog explanations as context, not proof.
- Do not mix benchmark numbers with incompatible settings or scales.
- Treat negative space as evidence: what a paper does not disclose matters.
- Track assumptions that would need experiments to verify.

## Transfer Gate

Before giving system advice, the learner should be able to answer:

- What is a paper fact?
- What is lineage context?
- What is the course reconstruction?
- What is unknown?
- What condition must hold for the idea to transfer?

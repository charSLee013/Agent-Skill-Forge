# Visible Evidence

Use visible evidence for `evidence-intensive`, `report-grade`, and `expert-audited` student pages.

## Rule

Clean student pages are not evidence-free pages. The main narrative should avoid path dumps and audit logs, but major claims must show why they are justified.

For each major claim, include a compact evidence block:

- Claim.
- Evidence type.
- Source summary.
- Boundary.
- Confidence.

Detailed anchors belong in the source matrix or collapsed appendix.

## Evidence Types

Use plain types:

- Source fact.
- Implementation observation.
- Experiment or validation result.
- External reference.
- Course reconstruction.
- Transfer guidance.
- Unknown or insufficient evidence.

## Boundary Language

State what the evidence does and does not establish.

Examples:

- "This supports the documented interface, not the private construction process."
- "This proves the observed behavior in this source, not every possible deployment."
- "This is a course reconstruction used to make the mechanism learnable."

## Failure Conditions

Block review when:

- Major claims appear without visible justification.
- Evidence exists only in backstage files.
- A learner must inspect paths or line numbers to understand why a claim is credible.
- A citation is used to hide inference.
